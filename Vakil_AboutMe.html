About Me

Me and my younger sister, Prianka.

I wish to pursue a career in Computer Science. For as long as I can remember, engineering as a whole has been my passion. I grew up with a natural flair for logic and math and in a household with both of my parents working as engineers. I found that their work inspired me. In addition, Disney animated movies were a huge, influential force in my life. The art of animation has always fascinated me. I hope that by combining my passions I will grow to become a software engineer or a 3D animator in the future. I want to help put smiles on children’s faces as Disney animated movies did for me. To aid myself, I plan to take as many engineering courses as possible throughout the remainder of my highschool career. The two that I have already taken not only greatly interested me, but also did a great deal to broaden my knowledge.

During my freshman year in highschool, I took the Project Lead the Way course Computer Science/Software Engineering and worked diligently to receive an A in both semesters. It was a very rigorous class that introduced me to the basics of coding and made me fluent in the programming languages HTML and CSS and Python. As a final project, we were instructed to form a group with any set of students and act as entrepreneurs. Our task was to design a product that we would pitch to our classes. The top three teams would then move on to participate in our school’s annual Entrepreneur Competition & Showcase. In our class, my group placed third for our Water Alarm. It was a prototype of a product that would be placed on a tap and set to emit audible noises if someone forgets to fully shut it off and water continues to flow for an excessive period of time. I helped program the device. For all of my efforts and evident display of interest in engineering, I was selected at the end of my freshmen year along with fifty-nine other students to be part of our school’s engineering academy. The academy provides mentorship opportunities to us in our junior year and priority into engineering classes and field trips. As a self-motivated learner, I took a Digital Art class at Mission College during the summer to get a taste of the basics of animation. This year I am currently enrolled in the Project Lead the War course Principles of Engineering, and my experience continues to be just as enjoyable.

In addition to these classes, I joined my schools VEX Robotics Club, Gael Force Robotics during my freshman year. I started out as a member on the development team, and even so it was initially incredibly daunting. Unlike myself, most of my teammates had participated in FIRST Lego League robotics in middle school. They were significantly more knowledgeable. I was also one of only five girls in the entire club of over thirty students. However, I remained persistent and as a result grew substantially as an engineer. The development team taught me a lot of basic engineering principles, as well the importance of communication skills and teamwork. I started sophomore year again on the development team, but was offered an internship on a competition team three months into the school year. After one month of being an intern, I became an official member of Team 5327C. This entire experience helped me realize is that although a task may seem initially daunting, with practice and hard work any barrier can be overcome. And that is the mindset that I intend to bring with me to any difficulty I am confronted with both at home and at school.

Thank you for taking the time to get know a bit about me and my interests. The rest of my portfolio features my projects and work as a highschool student in greater detail. Please feel free to explore and check out the Contact Me section if you ever need get ahold of me.
